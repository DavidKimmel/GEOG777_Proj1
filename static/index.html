About this Explorer

Data dictionary

  • Tract cancer rate — age-adjusted incidence per 100,000.
  • Well nitrate (mg/L) — source measurements aggregated to tracts.
  • IDW raster — nitrate surface interpolated from wells.
  • Jenks classes — 5-class natural breaks of tract cancer rates.
  • OLS summary — regression of cancer rate ~ nitrate (tract mean).

Why Jenks?

Jenks natural breaks maximize within-class similarity and between-class differences. For skewed rates, it’s often clearer than equal intervals.

Interpretation guidance

Small positive slope, low R² → weak linear association: higher nitrate tends to align with higher cancer rates, but the model explains little variance. Treat as a hypothesis generator, not a causal claim.
